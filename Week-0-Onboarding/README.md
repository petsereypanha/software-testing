# 🟣 Week 0 — Onboarding & Orientation

> **Duration:** 1–2 days before Week 1 begins
> **Goal:** Set the intern up for success by establishing expectations, providing tool access, and introducing the team.

---

## ✅ Onboarding Checklist

Work through every item on Day 1–2. Check off each task as completed.

### Administrative
- [ ] Sign NDA, contracts, and HR paperwork
- [ ] Receive and review the internship handbook
- [ ] Review the company code of conduct and safety policies
- [ ] Confirm work hours, remote/on-site policy, and communication norms

### Accounts & Access
- [ ] Company email set up and working
- [ ] Jira account created and added to team project
- [ ] Slack / Teams workspace access granted
- [ ] GitHub account created; added to relevant repositories
- [ ] Confluence / Notion access granted
- [ ] Postman account created
- [ ] VPN access configured (if required)

### Workstation Setup
- [ ] Laptop / workstation provisioned and usable
- [ ] Google Chrome installed (latest version)
- [ ] VS Code or PyCharm installed
- [ ] Git installed and configured (`git config --global user.name` and `user.email`)
- [ ] Python 3 installed and working (`python --version`)
- [ ] Access to staging/test environment configured and verified

### Social & Cultural
- [ ] Met direct mentor / supervisor
- [ ] Introduced to the core team (dev, product, design, QA)
- [ ] Attended or observed at least one team standup meeting
- [ ] Toured the office (physical) or virtual team intro call completed

## 📋 Expectations Alignment

Review these expectations with your mentor on Day 1.

### Work & Communication
- Daily standups: arrive on time, be prepared with 3-point update
- Slack etiquette: respond within 2 hours during work hours
- Meetings: camera on (if remote), take notes, ask questions
- Ask for help: if you're stuck for more than 30 minutes, ask — do not waste hours in silence

### Deliverables
- Every week ends with a submitted assignment (see each week's README)
- Late submissions must be communicated in advance — always better to flag early
- Quality over speed: a well-reasoned incomplete deliverable is better than a rushed complete one

### Feedback Culture
- Bi-weekly 1:1 reviews with mentor (Wednesdays + Fridays)
- All feedback is given to help you grow — receive it with an open mind
- You are encouraged to give feedback to your mentor too (how to improve your learning experience)

### The Right Mindset for QA
- **Be curious:** "What could go wrong here?" should be your constant instinct
- **Be specific:** Vague bug reports waste everyone's time; precision is kindness
- **Be thorough:** The happy path always works; the **edge cases** are where the real job is
- **No dumb questions:** Asking is always better than guessing wrong
